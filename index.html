    This Is Confirmation Bias

    Most people guess rules like "even numbers" or "increasing by 2"—and then only test sequences that confirm their hypothesis. They try 4-6-8, 10-12-14, 100-102-104... all variations of the same pattern.

    They rarely try sequences that could falsify their theory: 1-2-3, 5-10-99, or 3-2-1. But falsification is exactly what good reasoning requires.

    The sequences that don't fit are more informative than those that do. A single "doesn't fit" teaches you more than a dozen "fits."
